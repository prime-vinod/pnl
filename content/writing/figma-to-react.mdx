Most of my work starts in Figma and ends in a browser. The gap between the two is where design intent usually leaks out — spacing drifts, type scales get rounded off, breakpoints get guessed. Closing that gap is most of the job.

A few things I lean on:

- **Read the design system first, not the screens.** Tokens for color, type, and spacing tell you what's reusable before you write a single component.
- **Component-based architecture.** Small, composable pieces with Tailwind keep layouts pixel-perfect and the codebase clean as it scales.
- **Test where users actually are.** Cross-browser checks on Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge catch the things a single dev machine never will.
- **Performance is part of the design.** Lazy loading and render optimization keep the experience fast — a 30% load-time improvement is a feature, not a footnote.

Pixel-perfect isn't pixel-pushing. It's building the right abstractions so the design holds up everywhere.
